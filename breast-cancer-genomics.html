Breast Cancer Genomics: A Comprehensive Case Study

Overview

Breast cancer is a complex disease with significant genomic variability. This case study explores the genomic landscape of breast cancer, focusing on key genetic mutations and their clinical implications.

Key Genetic Mutations

  • BRCA1 and BRCA2: Crucial genes associated with hereditary breast cancer
  • TP53: Tumor suppressor gene frequently mutated in breast cancer
  • PIK3CA: Oncogene involved in cell growth and proliferation

Molecular Subtypes

Breast cancer can be classified into molecular subtypes based on genomic profile:

  • Luminal A
  • Luminal B
  • HER2-enriched
  • Basal-like (Triple-negative)

Precision Medicine Implications

Understanding the genomic profile allows for targeted therapies and personalized treatment strategies, improving patient outcomes and survival rates.
