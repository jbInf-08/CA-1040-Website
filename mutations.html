Understanding Cancer Mutations

Types of Genetic Mutations in Cancer

Mutations are changes in the DNA sequence that can lead to cancer development. Understanding these mutations is crucial for diagnosis and treatment.

Point Mutations

Small changes in a single nucleotide, which can alter protein function.

Insertions

Addition of extra DNA base pairs into the genome, potentially disrupting gene function.

Deletions

Removal of DNA segments, which can lead to loss of gene function.

Chromosomal Rearrangements

Large-scale changes in chromosome structure, including translocations and inversions.
