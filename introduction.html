Introduction to Cancer Genomics

What is Cancer Genomics?

Cancer genomics is a field of study that examines the genetic changes and molecular alterations underlying cancer development, progression, and treatment. By analyzing the entire genome of cancer cells, researchers can gain unprecedented insights into the disease's fundamental mechanisms.

Key Concepts

  • Genomic Alterations: Mutations, chromosomal rearrangements, and epigenetic changes that contribute to cancer development.
  • Precision Medicine: Tailoring treatments based on the unique genetic profile of an individual's cancer.
  • Biomarkers: Genetic indicators that can predict cancer risk, progression, and treatment response.

Research Approaches

Cancer genomics employs several cutting-edge research methodologies:

  1. Whole Genome Sequencing
  2. RNA Sequencing
  3. Epigenetic Profiling
  4. Single-Cell Genomics
