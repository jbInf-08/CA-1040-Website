Cancer Genomics Glossary

A

Allele
A variant form of a gene at a specific location (locus) on a chromosome.
Amplification
An increase in the number of copies of a specific DNA segment, often associated with cancer progression.

B

Base Pair (bp)
Two complementary nucleotides on opposite strands of DNA that are connected by hydrogen bonds.
Biomarker
A measurable indicator of biological state or condition, used in cancer for diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment selection.

C

CRISPR
A gene-editing technology that allows precise modifications to DNA sequences.
Chromosome
A structure containing DNA and protein, found in the cell nucleus, that carries genetic information.

G

Gene Expression
The process by which information from a gene is used to synthesize a functional gene product like a protein.
Genomics
The study of an organism's entire genome, including gene interactions and regulation.

M

Mutation
A change in the DNA sequence of a gene, which can be inherited or acquired.
Methylation
A chemical modification of DNA that can affect gene expression without changing the underlying DNA sequence.

N

Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS)
High-throughput DNA sequencing technologies that allow rapid sequencing of entire genomes.

O

Oncogene
A gene with the potential to cause cancer when mutated or overexpressed.

T

Tumor Suppressor Gene
A gene that prevents cells from growing and dividing too rapidly, helping to prevent cancer development.
