Precision Medicine: Targeted Cancer Therapies

Molecular Target Identification

Targeted therapies focus on specific genetic mutations or molecular pathways that drive cancer growth, enabling more precise and effective treatment strategies.

Classes of Targeted Therapies

These include monoclonal antibodies, small molecule inhibitors, immunotherapies, and gene therapies that specifically interact with cancer-related molecular targets.

Cancer Treatment Strategies

Treatment Description Use Case
Immunotherapy Boosts the immune system to recognize and attack cancer cells. Effective for melanoma and certain lung cancers.
Targeted Therapy Uses drugs designed to target specific genetic mutations in cancer cells. Commonly used in HER2-positive breast cancer and BRAF-mutated melanoma.
Gene Therapy Involves introducing or modifying genes in cancer cells to halt their growth. Experimental; used in clinical trials for leukemia and other cancers.
