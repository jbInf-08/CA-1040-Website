Lung Cancer Mutations: Genomic Insights

Genomic Landscape

Lung cancer exhibits complex genomic alterations that drive tumor progression and metastasis. Understanding these mutations is crucial for targeted therapies.

Significant Mutations

  • EGFR: Commonly mutated in non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC)
  • ALK: Rearrangements leading to oncogenic fusion proteins
  • ROS1: Rare but targetable mutations
  • TP53: Tumor suppressor gene frequently altered

Mutation Types

Lung cancer mutations can be classified into:

  • Point mutations
  • Gene rearrangements
  • Copy number variations
  • Chromosomal translocations

Clinical Implications

Identifying specific genomic alterations enables precision medicine approaches, including targeted therapies and immunotherapies tailored to individual genetic profiles.
