Understanding Somatic Mutations

What are Somatic Mutations?

Somatic mutations are genetic changes that occur in body cells during an individual's lifetime, not inherited from parents. These mutations can contribute to cancer development.

Types of Somatic Mutations

  • Point Mutations: Single nucleotide changes
  • Chromosomal Rearrangements: Large-scale genetic material exchanges
  • Copy Number Variations: Changes in gene count or structure

Causes and Implications

Somatic mutations can be caused by environmental factors like radiation, chemicals, and aging. Understanding these mutations helps develop targeted cancer therapies.
